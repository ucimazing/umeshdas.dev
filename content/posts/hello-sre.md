## The starting line

I'm Umesh. I joined Deloitte USI in September 2024 as an analyst. I spent my first year in a support role that had nothing to do with what I signed up for, then moved to a DevOps project working on Feature Flag rollouts and trunk-based development. That team got rolled off. Now I'm on bench.

Bench time is the most expensive free time of my life, so I'm not wasting it.

## The bet

For the next 31 weeks, I'm doing exactly one thing: building a Site Reliability Engineering portfolio strong enough to land a remote role at a company outside India.

Here's what that looks like, concretely:

- **Three production projects**, deployed and observable, with public dashboards
- **Three certifications**: AZ-104, CKA, HashiCorp Terraform Associate
- **Ten technical blog posts** — this is post #1
- **A revised resume** and an interview pipeline by November 2026

No side quests. No LeetCode in C++. No distractions into startup hustle videos or design tutorials. Just the plan, one sprint at a time.

## Why SRE

I'll write a full post on this later, but the short version: I love system design more than coding puzzles, I already have hands-on DevOps experience, and SRE is one of the most AI-resistant engineering roles that still pays well and hires remotely from India. It's the right bet for me.

## What this blog is

This blog will be honest engineering notes — not motivational content, not "10 tips" listicles. Things I'm actually building, actually breaking, and actually fixing. If you're on a similar path — service company, tier-3 college, feeling stuck — maybe the receipts help.

## What's next

The first real post will be about shipping a WebRTC file transfer app to production with full observability. That's the flagship portfolio project. I'll write as I build.

See you at post #2.

— Umesh
